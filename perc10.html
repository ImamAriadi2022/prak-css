Taman Kupu-Kupu Gita Persada

Indonesia, known as a mega biodiversity country, has more than 6,000 species of butterflies. Many of these species are on the brink of extinction. Gita Persada Butterfly Park was founded to counter the extinction. Gita Persada Butterfly Park, founded in 1997 by Anshori Djausal and Herawati Soekardi, serves as a model for conserving butterflies. The park has since been successful in conserving more than 180 species of Sumatran butterflies. This number increases every year along with research on butterfly food plants, micro-habitats, and reproduction. The park also becomes a place for education, especially environmental education, research, and tourist attraction.
